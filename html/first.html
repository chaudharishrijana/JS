Albert Einstein

Born:14 March 1879
Died: 18 April 1955

Known for:
Theory of relativity
Photoelectric effect

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist, widely acknowledged to be one of the greatest and most influential physicists of all time. Einstein is best known for developing the theory of relativity, but he also made important contributions to the development of quantum mechanics.

Early Life

Einstein was born in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg in the German Empire, on 14 March 1879.

Scientific Achievements

  • Special theory of relativity (1905)
  • General theory of relativity (1915)
  • Nobel Prize in Physics (1921) for the photoelectric effect

Legacy

Einstein's work has had a lasting impact on physics and the philosophy of science. His mass–energy equivalence formula E = mc2 is widely known as the world's most famous equation.
